1. Purpose

The purpose of this policy is to protect and secure Personally Identifiable Information (PII) in compliance with applicable local and international data protection laws and regulatory requirements. This includes ensuring the proper collection, processing, storage, and disposal of PII while implementing measures to safeguard privacy and minimise risks to data security.

2. Scope

This policy applies to all employees, contractors, and third parties who handle Personally Identifiable Information (PII) on behalf of ClassCover. It encompasses all aspects of data privacy and protection, including the collection, processing, storage, sharing, and disposal of PII.

3. Policy

  • Personal Identifiable Information shall refer to any information that shall be used to identify an individual, including but not limited to names, addresses, email addresses, and financial data.
  • ClassCover shall collect PII only when necessary for legitimate business purposes and shall inform individuals about the purpose of data collection and shall seek their consent when required by applicable laws.
  • ClassCover shall implement and maintain security controls to protect PII from unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, or destruction.
  • ClassCover is committed to using PII exclusively for the purposes for which it was collected, ensuring compliance with legal requirements and obtaining explicit consent.
  • ClassCover shall not share PII with third parties unless it is necessary for the intended purpose or required by law.
  • ClassCover shall obtain explicit, informed, and unambiguous consent from data subjects before sharing Personal Identifiable Information (PII) with third parties such as Braze.
  • ClassCover shall clearly inform data subjects that:
    • Their personal data will be shared with a third-party processor for marketing and communication purposes.
    • Their data may be transferred to and stored outside the EU region (e.g., Australia), constituting a cross-border data transfer.
  • ClassCover shall ensure that:
    • Consent is obtained through clear opt-in mechanisms (no pre-ticked boxes).
    • Consent is separate from general terms and conditions.
    • Records of consent are maintained for audit and compliance purposes.
  • Data subjects shall have the right to:
    • Withdraw consent at any time.
    • Opt out of marketing communications without impacting core services.
  • ClassCover shall ensure that only the minimum necessary data is shared with third parties (data minimisation principle).
  • Once the retention period has expired, ClassCover shall securely dispose of PII collected, ensuring that it cannot be accessed or reconstructed.
  • Individuals have the right to access their PII held by ClassCover and may request corrections if the information is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • In the event of a data breach involving PII, ClassCover shall handle the incident in accordance with the Incident Management Policy and Procedure, ensuring timely reporting, investigation, and remediation.
  • ClassCover shall provide regular training to employees and contractors on data privacy and security best practices, ensuring they are informed of their responsibilities and equipped to handle PII in a secure and compliant manner.
  • This policy shall be subject to periodic review and update to ensure it remains effective and aligned with changing regulatory requirements.

4. Procedure

4.1 Personal Data

Personal data refers to any information that can identify an individual, either directly or indirectly. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Teacher name
  • Contact details
  • Phone number
  • Location
  • Photos
  • Information related to their experience and reference contact details

4.2 Special Data

Special data is a subset of personal data that is considered particularly sensitive and deserving of extra protection under data protection laws. This type of data typically includes information related to an individual's:

  • Health: Medical records, information about disabilities, or details about an individual's physical or mental health.
  • Racial or Ethnic Origin: Information indicating a person's racial or ethnic background.
  • Religious or Philosophical Beliefs: Data revealing an individual's religious beliefs, philosophical convictions, or membership in religious or philosophical organisations.
  • Political Opinions: Information related to an individual's political opinions, affiliations, or activities.
  • Sexual Orientation and Sex Life: Information regarding an individual's sexual orientation, sexual activities, or preferences.

Special data requires additional protection due to its sensitive nature, as mishandling can lead to discrimination or harm.

4.3 Consent Form

A Consent Form will be used to obtain clear and explicit permission from individuals to collect and process their personal data. It outlines what personal data will be collected, the specific purpose for its use, and any third parties who may access it.

Individuals must provide their explicit consent by signing the form or taking an affirmative action to confirm they understand and agree to the data collection and processing as outlined.

Consent forms shall explicitly include:

  • Disclosure of data sharing with authorised third-party service providers (e.g., Braze).
  • Information about data hosting and possible cross-border data transfers (e.g., data hosted in Australia).
  • Purpose of sharing (e.g., marketing, communication, or user engagement).

Consent shall be:

  • Captured via affirmative action (e.g., checkbox / opt-in mechanism).
  • Logged and securely stored as evidence of consent for compliance and audit purposes.

Personal data will only be processed for the specific purpose stated in the Consent Form. ClassCover will ensure that data is used exclusively as described and within the scope of the consent given.

4.4 Privacy Notice

A Privacy Notice will be provided by ClassCover to inform individuals about how their personal data will be collected, processed, and protected. It will outline the organisation's data handling practices, including the purpose of data processing, retention periods, and individuals' rights regarding their data.

4.5 Personal Data Analysis

Division heads, in coordination with their teams, shall accurately and comprehensively complete the DPIA sheet for their respective processing activities. The sheet contains the following fields:

  • PII Data: Specifies the type of personal data processed by ClassCover, such as teacher profile details, contact information, qualifications, background-check documents (e.g., WWCC), booking records, payment details, or usage logs.
  • Data Owner: Identifies the responsible role within ClassCover (e.g., Head of Operations, IT Manager, Privacy Officer) accountable for ensuring lawful and secure processing of the data.
  • Department of Processing: Indicates which internal department processes the data, such as Operations (bookings), HR (recruitment), Finance (payments), IT (platform hosting), or Customer Support.
  • Project or Business Process: Describes the specific ClassCover activity or system where the data is used, such as teacher onboarding, casual booking management, subscription billing, or background verification.
  • Purpose of Processing: Defines why the data is processed, for example facilitating bookings between teachers and schools, verifying credentials, processing subscriptions, ensuring child-safety compliance, or improving platform functionality.
  • Is it Special Data: Indicates whether the processing involves special category data under GDPR, such as health information or criminal background checks (e.g., Working with Children Check), which require enhanced protection.
  • Basis of Processing the Data: Specifies the lawful basis relied upon, such as contractual necessity (providing the platform), legal obligation (child protection laws), legitimate interests (platform improvement), or explicit consent (special category data).
  • Source of Data Collection: Identifies whether data is collected directly from teachers or schools, through uploaded documents, third-party verification services, analytics tools, or publicly available professional registers.
  • Data Subject: Specifies the category of individuals whose data is processed, such as teachers, school administrators, contractors, employees, or platform users.
  • Location of Data Storage: Identifies the technical environment where the data is hosted, such as secure cloud infrastructure (e.g., AWS or equivalent hosting provider).
  • Country of Data Stored In: Specifies the country where the hosting servers are located (e.g., Australia or other jurisdictions), relevant for assessing cross-border transfer risks.
  • Data Retention: Defines how long personal data is retained, such as during active account usage and for a defined period after deactivation to meet legal, audit, or regulatory requirements.
  • Data Deletion: Explains how data is securely deleted, anonymised, or archived once it is no longer required, in accordance with ClassCover's Data Retention and Disposal Policy.
  • Is the Data Transferred with Any Third Party: Indicates whether data is shared with cloud providers, payment gateways, background-check agencies, analytics providers, or regulatory authorities.
  • Risk Statement: Describes the privacy risk associated with the processing activity, such as unauthorised access to teacher credentials, exposure of child-safety documentation, or misuse of booking data.
  • Probability Rating to Breach: Assesses the likelihood of a breach occurring, based on system exposure, volume of data, and existing controls.
  • Impact Rating: Evaluates the potential harm if a breach occurs, including reputational damage, regulatory fines, impact on schools or teachers, and child-safety implications.
  • Risk Score: Represents the calculated overall risk level, derived from the probability and impact assessment.
  • Mitigation Action: Lists the safeguards implemented by ClassCover, such as encryption, role-based access controls, background-check verification controls, audit logging, vendor agreements, staff training, and incident-response procedures.

4.6 Third-Party Data Sharing

ClassCover may share limited personal data (e.g., mobile numbers, email IDs) with authorised third-party service providers (e.g., Braze) for purposes such as marketing, communication, or user engagement.

Before sharing data:

  • Explicit consent shall be obtained from users.
  • Data shall be limited to necessary fields only (data minimisation).

ClassCover shall ensure:

  • A valid Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is in place with such third parties.
  • The third party implements appropriate technical and organisational security controls.

4.7 Cross-Border Data Transfer

Personal data shared with authorised third-party service providers (e.g., Braze) may be stored and processed in other jurisdictions (e.g., Australia).

ClassCover shall:

  • Inform users about cross-border data transfers during consent collection.
  • Ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws (e.g., GDPR).
  • Implement appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) and vendor risk assessments.

5. Data Protection Impact Assessment

5.1 Identify Risk Scenarios

The identification of risks will be performed by a combination of group discussion and interview with interested parties. Such interested parties will normally include (where possible):

  • Department Head responsible for each activity
  • Representatives of the people that normally carry out each aspect of the activity
  • Providers of the inputs to the activity (including, where appropriate, the data subject)
  • Recipients of the outputs of the activity
  • Appropriate third parties with relevant knowledge
  • Representatives of those providing supporting services and resources to the activity
  • Any other party that is felt to provide useful input to the risk identification process

Identified risks will be recorded in the DPIA with as full a description as possible that allows the likelihood and impact of the risk to be assessed.

5.2 Analyse the Risks

Risk analysis involves assigning a numerical value to the likelihood and impact of a risk. These values are then multiplied to arrive at a classification level of High, Medium, or Low.

Likelihood Rating:

RatingDescription
Rare (1)The threat has very rare probability of occurrence — at least once in five years.
Unlikely (2)Probability of the threat compromising the vulnerability is once in three years.
Possible (3)Threat can occur sometimes — at least once in a year.
Likely (4)The threat has high probability of occurrence of half-yearly once.
Probable (5)The threat has very high probability of occurrence of quarterly once.

Impact Rating:

RatingDescription
Very Low (1)Little or no effect if the personal data is compromised.
Low (2)Some effect if the personal data is compromised. (Negligible internal operation loss.)
Medium (3)Considerable effect if the personal data is compromised. (Essential internal operation loss.)
High (4)Great effect if the personal data is compromised. (May lead to breach in SLA and cause nominal financial impact.)
Very High (5)Catastrophic effect if the personal data is compromised. (May lead to financial loss for customers and reputational or financial loss for ClassCover, or legal action.)

Risk Rating = Impact Rating × Likelihood Rating

5.3 Risk Classification

Based on the score calculated by multiplying likelihood and impact ratings, each risk is classified as follows:

  • HIGH: 12 or more
  • MEDIUM: 5 to 10 inclusive
  • LOW: 1 to 4 inclusive

5.4 Risk Threshold Level

Based on the above matrix the risk can be ranked on a scale of 1 to 25. The threshold level of risk rating is ≤12. The result of the Data Protection Impact Assessment will be reported to the DPO with its recommendation regarding the risk threshold level. Once approved, all risks above the threshold level will require a treatment plan monitored by the DPO. All risks above the threshold limit that cannot be mitigated will be considered as residual risks and must be approved by the DPO.

5.5 Define Risk Treatment Plan

For risks agreed to be above the threshold for acceptance by ClassCover, treatment options will be explored. The overall intention of risk treatment is to reduce the classification of a risk to an acceptable level. Where treatment reduces the score but not the classification (e.g., from 10 to 5 — both Medium), the organisation may decide to accept the risk, and such decisions should be recorded with a suitable explanation.

5.6 Obtain Management Approval for Residual Risks

At each stage of the DPIA process, management will be kept informed of progress and decisions made, including formal sign-off of proposed residual risks. Management will approve the DPIA report and will consider to what extent the report should be made public. Sign-off will be indicated according to ClassCover documentation standards. In addition to overall management approval, the acceptance or treatment of each risk should be signed off by the relevant Department owner.

5.7 Prior Consultation with Supervisory Authority

If the results of the DPIA indicate a high level of risk prior to the identified controls being implemented, the supervisory authority must be consulted before any processing takes place. The following information must be provided:

  • Details of the respective responsibilities of the controller, joint controllers, and processors, where applicable
  • Purposes and means of the processing
  • The controls that will be implemented to protect the data
  • Contact details for the DPO
  • A copy of the impact assessment report

The supervisory authority has eight weeks (extendable by a further six weeks) to provide a judgement on the proposed processing and, if appropriate, give details of what must be done to make the processing acceptable.

5.8 Risk Treatment and Monitoring

Data Protection Impact Assessment is the overall responsibility of the DPO wherever applicable. The DPO should implement controls to mitigate the identified risk in the processing of the personal data. Appropriate controls shall be put in place to ensure that all risks above the threshold level are mitigated and proper security is provided for all personal data collected, stored, and processed. This DPIA sheet shall be reviewed by the DPO on a yearly basis, or on a need-to-verify basis in case of changes made inside the organisation.

5.9 Data Processing Agreement

A Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is a legal contract that outlines the responsibilities and obligations of two key entities: the data controller and the data processor.

  • Data Controller: The entity that determines the purposes and means of processing personal data. Data controllers have specific legal obligations, including ensuring that the processing of personal data is lawful and that data subjects' rights are respected.
  • Data Processor: The entity that processes personal data on behalf of the data controller. They act based on instructions provided by the data controller and are responsible for implementing appropriate security measures to protect the data.

Key elements typically included in a DPA include:

  • The nature and purpose of data processing.
  • The types of personal data involved.
  • The obligations and responsibilities of the data processor, including data security measures.
  • The data controller's right to audit the data processor's activities.
  • Provisions for the data processor to assist the data controller in responding to data subject requests and data breaches.
  • Terms related to sub-processing (if the data processor uses subcontractors).
  • Provisions for the termination of the agreement.

Where Personal Data is transferred outside the country or jurisdiction of the data controller (e.g., to authorised third-party service providers such as Braze with data hosted in other jurisdictions such as Australia), ClassCover shall ensure that a valid cross-border data transfer mechanism (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses or equivalent safeguards) is established and that such transfers comply with applicable data protection laws.

6. Data Subject Rights

Data subjects are individuals whose personal data is collected, processed, or stored by organisations. Under data protection regulations, data subjects have the following rights:

  • Right to Access: Data subjects have the right to obtain confirmation from the data controller as to whether their personal data is being processed and, if so, access to that data and related information.
  • Right to Rectification: Data subjects can request the correction of inaccurate or incomplete personal data.
  • Right to Erasure (Right to Be Forgotten): Data subjects have the right to request the deletion of their personal data under specific circumstances, such as when the data is no longer necessary for the purposes for which it was collected.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: Data subjects can request the limitation of the processing of their personal data under certain conditions, such as during the verification of the accuracy of the data.
  • Right to Data Portability: Data subjects can request to receive their personal data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format, allowing them to transfer it to another data controller.
  • Right to Object to Processing: Data subjects have the right to object to the processing of their personal data, particularly for direct marketing purposes or when the processing is based on legitimate interests.
  • Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making and Profiling: Data subjects have the right not to be subject to decisions based solely on automated processing, including profiling, if these decisions significantly affect them.
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: If processing is based on consent, data subjects have the right to withdraw their consent at any time without affecting the lawfulness of processing based on consent before its withdrawal.
  • Right to Lodge a Complaint: Data subjects can lodge a complaint with a data protection authority if they believe their data protection rights have been violated.

Data subjects may submit requests using our Data Subject Rights Request Form or by emailing the designated address provided publicly. The DPO will undertake the verification of the data subject and their request, aiming to respond within 30 calendar days. Where the request cannot be addressed within this timeframe due to the need for data gathering, the DPO will promptly communicate the delay to the data subject. All data subject requests received will be recorded in the Data Subject Request Register by the DPO, with regular updates on their status.

7. Data Breach or Incident

Any incident or data breach that involves personal information will be managed following the incident management procedure. In the event of an incident or data breach involving personal information, affected data subjects will be promptly informed about the incident and will receive ongoing updates on how it is being handled by the DPO.

8. Data Protection Officer (DPO)

ClassCover shall appoint a Data Protection Officer (DPO) where required under applicable data protection laws. The DPO shall possess expert knowledge of data protection law, regulatory requirements, and organisational processing activities.

  • The DPO shall operate independently, report directly to the highest level of management, and shall not receive instructions regarding the performance of their statutory tasks. The DPO shall not hold any role that results in a conflict of interest.
  • The DPO shall inform and advise management, employees, Controllers, and Processors of their obligations under applicable data protection laws, including updates in regulatory requirements and best practices.
  • The DPO shall advise on and oversee Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs), including methodology, risk evaluation, mitigation measures, and management of residual risks.
  • The DPO shall oversee the handling of data subject requests to ensure responses are accurate, transparent, timely, and compliant, including appropriate identity verification prior to disclosure.
  • The DPO shall advise management on breach risks, oversee breach response coordination, and support compliance with regulatory notification obligations, including the 72-hour reporting requirement where applicable.
  • The DPO shall provide guidance on processor selection, review of Data Processing Agreements, cross-border data transfers, and oversight of third parties handling Personal Data.
  • The DPO shall promote data protection awareness across the organisation and oversee regular training programmes appropriate to roles and risk levels.
  • The DPO shall have access to all necessary information, systems, personnel, and resources required to perform their duties effectively, and shall be authorised to communicate with supervisory authorities where required.

To contact the DPO, email info@classcover.com.au — Attention: DPO.

9. Application Privacy Policy

The Application Privacy Policy defines how Personal Data is collected, used, stored, protected, and shared within the ClassCover application. ClassCover shall ensure that all Personal Data collected through the application is processed lawfully, fairly, and for legitimate business purposes. The application policy shall clearly explain what data is collected, why it is collected, how it is used, retention periods, user rights, and security measures implemented. The policy shall describe data sharing practices, including third-party processors and cross-border transfers where applicable. The Application Privacy Policy shall be reviewed at least annually or whenever there is a significant change in functionality, processing activity, or legal requirement.

10. Website Privacy Policy

The Website Privacy Policy defines how Personal Data is collected and managed through the ClassCover website. ClassCover shall provide clear information on the website regarding data collection methods such as forms, cookies, analytics, and user accounts. The website policy shall explain the purpose of data collection, lawful basis, retention periods, user rights, and how users can contact ClassCover regarding privacy concerns. The policy shall disclose any third-party integrations, tracking technologies, or data transfers. The Website Privacy Policy shall be reviewed at least annually or when website features, tracking tools, or legal requirements change.

11. Terms and Conditions

The Terms and Conditions document defines the contractual obligations between ClassCover and its users, including data protection responsibilities. The Terms and Conditions shall include a clear clause explaining how Personal Data is processed in connection with the services provided. It shall outline user responsibilities, acceptable use, data ownership, and limitations of liability related to data protection. It shall reference the applicable Privacy Policy and Data Processing Agreement where relevant. The Terms and Conditions shall be reviewed annually or upon significant service, legal, or regulatory updates.

12. Data Controller Procedure

The Data Controller Procedure defines how ClassCover determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data and ensures lawful, fair, and transparent processing activities. The procedure shall outline responsibilities of the Data Controller, including lawful basis identification, purpose limitation, data minimisation, accuracy, storage limitation, and accountability requirements. It shall describe how Personal Data is collected, used, stored, shared with processors, retained, and securely disposed of in accordance with applicable data protection laws. It shall define mechanisms for handling Data Subject Rights, data breaches, third-party oversight, documentation requirements, and regulatory cooperation. The Data Controller Procedure shall be reviewed annually or upon significant legal, regulatory, organisational, or processing changes.
