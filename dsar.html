Data Subject Request

Version 1.0  ·  Effective date: 17 February 2026  ·  Classification: Public

Your data rights under GDPR

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to know what personal data ClassCover holds about you — and to request that we access, correct, restrict, or delete it. This page explains how to submit a formal request.

What can you request?

Access

Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.

Correction

Ask us to correct inaccurate or incomplete personal data.

Deletion

Request that we erase your personal data where legally permitted.

Restriction of processing

Ask us to limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.

Data portability

Receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format.

Objection

Object to certain types of processing, including direct marketing.

Withdraw consent

Withdraw consent you previously gave, at any time.

Automated decisions

Request human review of any automated decisions that affect you.

How to submit a request

1

Download the form

Download the official ClassCover Data Subject Request Form below. It's an Excel file you can fill in on your computer.

2

Fill in your details and request type

Complete Parts 1–5 of the form — your contact details, what you're requesting, a brief description, identity verification, and your declaration.

3

Email the completed form to us

Send the filled-in form to info@classcover.com.au with the subject line "Data Subject Request". Please include a copy of a valid photo ID so we can verify your identity.

4

We respond within 30 days

Under GDPR, we are required to respond to your request within one calendar month. We will confirm receipt of your request and keep you informed of progress.

Identity verification: To protect your data, we must verify your identity before processing any request. Please attach a copy of a valid photo ID (e.g. passport or driving licence) when emailing your form. We will not process requests where we cannot confirm the identity of the requester.

Right to complain: If you are unhappy with how we handle your request, or believe your data is being processed unlawfully, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Ireland, this is the Data Protection Commission (DPC).

Contact our DPO
For any questions about your data rights, contact us at info@classcover.com.au — Attention: DPO.
